Refined — single Display flyout, no rail
Two changes from 2a/2b: the layout switcher is gone from the viewport — it's workspace chrome and belongs on the pane divider we designed in the workspace-panes work — and the left rail is dropped. With only Output, Scale, and Light to house, a persistent rail isn't justified; a single Display button opening one frost flyout carries it. The flyout keeps 2a's exact content and styling, so if the toolset grows later (extra lights, masks, measurement) it promotes to a left rail with no rework — same flyout, re-anchored. 3a below; the earlier explorations remain beneath for reference.

YMIR · VIEWPORT · DISPLAY CONTROLS
Viewport controls — two directions
Both keep the same essentials on the render — projection 3D/2D, the fidelity readout, and the layout switcher — and tuck light, exaggeration, scale, and the output picker into a flyout. 2D and 3D keep separate state; light is per-projection; the output picker mirrors the inspector's. They differ in how much lives as persistent chrome: 2a docks a vertical toolbar to the left edge (your stated intent); 2b stays overlay-native — almost no chrome, one popover on demand.
2a Left-edge vertical toolbar SUPERSEDED BY 3A
A persistent icon rail docked to the viewport's left edge — matching the left/right panel “scope-by-side” rule. Each icon opens a frost flyout with World-Settings-style sections. Predictable, always in the same spot, survives a short viewport (rail collapses to icons only).
